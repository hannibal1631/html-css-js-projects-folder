DAVID GOGGINS

The Man Who Conquered the Impossible

Timeline of David Goggins' Life:

  • 1975: Born in Buffalo, New York.
  • 1994: Enlisted in the United States Air Force and trained as a Tactical Air Control Party (TACP) member.
  • 1999: Discharged from the Air Force due to a medical issue. Goggins then gained significant weight and faced depression.
  • 2001: Decided to change his life and joined the United States Navy. Despite being overweight and out of shape, he pushed through and became a Navy SEAL.
  • 2005: Graduated from Basic Underwater Demolition/SEAL (BUD/S) training with Class 235, earning the SEAL Trident.
  • 2006: Completed the San Diego One Day 24-hour ultramarathon to qualify for the Badwater Ultramarathon. His performance caught the attention of the ultrarunning community.
  • 2007: Completed the Badwater Ultramarathon, a 135-mile race through Death Valley, under extreme conditions.
  • 2008: Set a world record for the most pull-ups done in 24 hours with 4,030 pull-ups.
  • 2010s: Continued participating in numerous ultramarathons, triathlons, and endurance races, gaining recognition for his mental toughness and physical endurance.
  • 2018: Published his memoir, "Can't Hurt Me: Master Your Mind and Defy the Odds," which became a bestseller and inspired many with his story of overcoming adversity.
  • Present: Continues to motivate and inspire people through public speaking, social media, and his continued participation in extreme endurance events.
